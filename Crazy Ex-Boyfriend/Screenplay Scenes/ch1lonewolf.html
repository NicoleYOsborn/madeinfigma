FADE IN

EXT. DUSTY WESTERN TOWN - DAY

Dust swirls in the hot breeze as REMUS LUPIN, clad in worn leather, rides into the weathered town on a weary horse. His eyes scan the seemingly abandoned streets.

CUT TO:

INT. SALOON - DAY

Remus enters a dimly lit SALOON. The patrons go silent, their eyes locked onto him. A hushed exchange of words ripples through the room.

RANDOM PATRON 1:

(whispers)

I think that’s Lone-wolf Lupin, the loneliest sonuvabitch in the West.

Remus disregards the comments, approaches the bar, and orders a whiskey. He carries it to a shadowy BOOTH in the corner.

A few minutes pass, and a STRIKINGLY HANDSOME MAN slides into the booth across from Remus.

THE STRANGER:

I hear you’re a traveler.

REMUS LUPIN:

That’s right. I’m lookin’ for somethin’ I just haven’t been able to find yet.

THE STRANGER:

Might be that we’re lookin’ for the same thing.

REMUS LUPIN:

And what’s that?

THE STRANGER:

(smirking)

I’ll know it when I see it. I’m headin’ out west. Maybe you’d like to journey together?

Remus, with a hint of defiance, spits tobacco juice into a SPITTOON.

REMUS LUPIN:

(gruff)

Maybe you haven’t heard. I’m a lone wolf. Who are you, anyway?

The stranger flashes a charming grin and refills both their whiskey glasses. He raises his glass.

THE STRANGER:

(confident)

They call me The Black Dog. Maybe it’s time you found your pack, Mr. Wolf. I’m headin’ out tomorrow at daybreak. Hope to see you then.

With that, The Black Dog saunters out through the SWINGING DOORS, leaving Remus Lupin in the shadowy embrace of the saloon, contemplating his next move under the relentless Western sun.
